What Happened Last Night (8:30pm → 11pm)

Why you got stopped

Your session bucket ran out. You'd been working hard and used it all up in one 5-hour window. Even though your weekly bucket had loads left, the session bucket is a separate, smaller limit.

The session bucket started refilling the moment it ran out. It takes up to 5 hours to fully refill. That's why you could work again around 11pm — it had refilled enough by then.

Think of it like a water cooler at work. The big tank (weekly) has plenty of water. But the cup (session) can only hold so much. When the cup is empty, you have to wait for it to refill before you can drink again — even though the big tank is full.

Why last night was probably a one-off

Last night you were likely in one very long chat doing lots of heavy work (brand tokens, constructors, lots of code). Long conversations use more of your session bucket because Claude has to re-read the entire conversation history with every message you send.

Today's session (building the schema) used only 29% — because we kept it focused on one topic. Starting new chats for each piece of work is the single best thing you can do to avoid hitting the session limit.

3 Simple Rules to Never Get Stopped Again

Rule 1: Start a new chat for each task

Don't keep one mega-conversation going for hours. Each new chat starts with a fresh, empty session. I remember everything through my memory system anyway.

Rule 2: Your dinner break is perfectly timed

If you start at 11:45, your session refills around 4:45pm. Your dinner at 5pm means you come back to a completely full bucket. It's like a natural reset.

Rule 3: If you do get stopped, check the timer

Your settings page shows "Resets in X hr Y min". You don't always have to wait the full 5 hours — it might only be 30 minutes away from refilling. Last night you may have only needed to wait an hour, not until 11pm.
